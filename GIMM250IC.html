GIMM 250 Interactive Comic

For our project we got death, and quantum immortality. Our plan for the comic panels was to keep the art style simple, black and white. We went with a lot of amplification through simplification for our project by keeping the story and themes simplistic and easy to understand.

The game portion of our comic has a simplistic art style but rather hard levels that are meant to force the player to eventually quit and give up. And as such their player goes with death to what ever lies after. We decided to make the player character fire as fire traditionally represents life and to in the game represent the player’s soul. The player can clone themselves which is in itself a representation of the many worlds theory, and superposition, we also have it so that the player can light braziers but when they light them they die and a clone is spawned in, this represents the fact that according to quantum mechanics you can not perceive yourself as having died.

The animation serves to introduce the player to the key concepts of our game, with the professor’s name Mortem being the Latin word for death, which is meant to be symbolic of how are game goes into death and how he is explain how death works in our game. In the animation the professor explains Schrodinger’s cat, which is basically what the player is in the game.

In one of the levels I did, the player has to instead of getting themselves to the game exit area get their clone to the exit, this is in it’s very essence an example of superposition and the many worlds theory due to the players actions in one portion of the game allowing the other part of the level to advance until ultimately the player manages to get the clone to the exit completing the level.
